Global Sales Key insights

This was calculated primarily through Excel functions:

  • AVERAGE() to calculate the mean
  • MEDIAN() to calculate the median
  • MODE.SNGL() to calculate the single mean within entire column of global sales data
  • MAX({column of data})-MIN({column})to calculate the range
  • STDEV() to calculate the standard deviation

Overall, global sales data was characterized by a strong right-skew. That is, there are few high-value items that increase the average significantly, while most of the items scored significantly less global sales comparatively.

Regarding the degree of skewedness, it can be observed that the mean (0.54) is significantly higher than both the median (0.17) and the mode (0.02). Such a difference indicates that the data is highly right-skewed. A small number of games, such as Wii Sports (the highest global sales at 82.74 million copies), Super Mario Bros (second highest value at 40.24 million copies), and Mario Kart Wii (third highest value at 35.82 million copies) excessively increase the average value. On the other hand, median, the middle value remains at only 0.17 million, indicating that half of all games were sold less than 0.17 million copies.
